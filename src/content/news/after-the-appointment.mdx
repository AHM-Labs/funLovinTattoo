# Optimal Tattoo Aftercare

The longevity and vibrancy of your tattoo depend entirely on what happens in the first few weeks. Follow these rules to ensure your piece heals flawlessly.

## 1. Wrapping: Second Skin vs. Traditional
- **Second Skin (Recommended)**: We use a medical-grade, transparent bandage (e.g., Tegaderm, Saniderm) that is breathable and waterproof. Keep it on for **3 to 5 days** for the best healing.
  - *Removal*: If it begins to peel, let us know. Otherwise, let it stay for the full duration.
- **Traditional Wrapping**: In cases where Second Skin is not suitable (e.g., skin sensitivity or specific placements), we will use a traditional wrap. Keep this on for **2 to 4 hours**.
  - *Next Step*: Once removed, wash immediately with mild, fragrance-free soap.

## 2. Cleaning and Drying
- **Wash Gently**: Use only your clean hands and lukewarm water. No washcloths or sponges!
- **Fragrance-Free Soap**: Use a mild, unscented, anti-bacterial soap.
- **Pat Dry**: Use a clean paper towel to pat the area dry. Do not rub.

## 3. Moisturizing
Apply a **very thin layer** of recommended aftercare cream twice a day. The tattoo should be moisturized but still able to 'breathe'. If it looks shiny, you've used too much.

## 4. Long-Term Maintenance
- **Airing it Out**: After the initial healing, let your tattoo breathe as much as possible. Wear loose, cotton clothing.
- **Sun Protection**: UV rays are the greatest enemy of tattoo vibrancy. Use sunscreen once the tattoo is fully healed (after 3-4 weeks).
- **Don't Pick!**: It will itch. This is normal. Picking or scratching will pull the ink out of your skin.

***

> **Question?**: If you have any concerns about redness, excessive swelling, or unusual pain, please contact us immediately or consult a medical professional.
